# Prestige / Social Status — Doctrine

## Core Principle

Prestige in DisciplineOS must feel earned, visible, aspirational, and safe. It connects real effort to visible progress to meaningful identity to social signaling — without becoming noisy, fake, or toxic.

## Goal A — Prestige Must Feel Earned

Status cannot feel like empty decoration. Prestige comes from meaningful combinations of:
- Proof-backed discipline (consistency, trust, proof quality)
- Real growth (level, skill mastery, milestones)
- Identity development (room, wardrobe, visual evolution, history)
- Status asset ownership (earned through discipline-backed economy)
- Recognition (titles, badges, challenge completions)

No single dimension alone creates high prestige. A user who only spends stays below "established." A user who only grinds stays below "elite." Only balanced, sustained effort across multiple dimensions reaches the top.

## Goal B — Prestige Must Be Socially Legible

A user viewing another user's showcase should understand:
- This person has built something real
- This person has progressed meaningfully
- This person has discipline, style, and identity weight

Without needing to decode scores or numbers. Prestige bands (emerging → iconic) communicate status at a glance.

## Goal C — Prestige Must Stay Premium

Recognition is selective and meaningful:
- Maximum 6 recognition slots displayed at once
- Maximum 5 showcase highlights
- Maximum 3 featured milestones
- No badge spam, no trivial distinctions cluttering the surface

Quality over quantity. Scarcity of attention creates real status.

## Goal D — Prestige Must Stay Safe

The system avoids:
- Shame-heavy comparison (no raw public scores, no exact rankings)
- Pay-to-win-only status (spending weight capped at 15% of total)
- Toxic public ranking pressure (bands only, no leaderboards)
- Privacy overexposure (per-field visibility scopes)
- Status inflation (bounded scores, named bands, no endless number growth)

## Goal E — Prestige Must Strengthen Identity

Status connects: real effort → visible progress → meaningful identity → social signaling

Each prestige band has emotional framing that reinforces identity:
- "You're building the foundation" (emerging)
- "This is who you are now" (established)
- "Earned, not given" (elite)
- "You've become the example" (iconic)

## Goal F — Prestige Must Be Extendable

Phase 36 creates a foundation for:
- Future circles recognition and prestige cards
- Future seasonal/live-ops prestige events
- Future social-safe sharing of prestige surfaces
- Future prestige history tracking
- Future dynamic band maintenance (bands can shift if signals decay)

## Anti-Patterns (Never Do)

1. Never let pure spending define prestige
2. Never let pure grind alone define prestige
3. Never expose trust scores, raw proof content, or penalty history publicly
4. Never create toxic leaderboards with exact rankings
5. Never manufacture urgency around prestige advancement
6. Never inflate badges or recognitions for engagement
7. Never shame users for lower bands
8. Never force social visibility — sharing is always intentional

## Signal Weight Doctrine

| Family | Weight | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Discipline | 30% | Core product value — consistency and proof quality |
| Growth | 25% | Progression and mastery — the RPG backbone |
| Identity | 15% | Visual expression and history — personal brand |
| Status Asset | 15% | Economy participation — but capped to prevent pay-to-win |
| Recognition | 15% | Social proof — badges, titles, circles |

This weighting ensures discipline + growth dominate (55% combined), while identity/assets/recognition add depth without dominating.
